Marie Curie Honour's

Marie Curie received numerous prestigious honours. She was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903 and the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1911 by the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences for her groundbreaking work on radioactivity and her discovery of radium and polonium. Additionally, in 1903, she received the Davy Medal from the Royal Society of London and the Matteucci Medal from the Italian Society of Sciences in 1904. In 1921, the Franklin Institute honored her with the Elliott Cresson Medal for her contributions to science. She was also awarded the Légion d'Honneur by the French Government in 1920 and the Gold Medal of the Royal Belgian Academy in 1911.

Abdus Salam Honours

Abdus Salam received numerous prestigious honours during his career. He was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1979 for his contributions to the unification of the weak force and electromagnetic interaction. In the same year, he also received the Copley Medal from the Royal Society of London. Salam was honored with the Max Planck Medal in 1984 for his achievements in theoretical physics. Additionally, he received the Lazare Carnot Prize in 1990 from the French Academy of Sciences. He was also awarded the King Faisal International Prize for Science in 1981 and the Humboldt Prize from the Alexander von Humboldt Foundation in the same year.

Aziz Sancar Honour's

Aziz Sancar has earned a range of prestigious honors throughout his distinguished career. In 2015, he was jointly awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry with Tomas Lindahl and Paul L. Modrich for their pioneering work on DNA repair mechanisms. In 2012, Sancar received the LOréal-UNESCO For Women in Science Award in recognition of his significant contributions to molecular biology. He was also honored with the 2015 Shaw Prize in Life Science and Medicine for his transformative research on DNA repair. In 2016, Sancar was presented with the Presidential Medal of Freedom, one of the highest civilian awards in the United States, for his exceptional scientific achievements. Additionally, he was recognized in Turkey with the 2016 Turkish Presidential Culture and Art Grand Award for his remarkable contributions to science.
